## What the Private Chair Is

The Sovereign Private Chair is the **highest-trust, closest-proximity operating partner**
within the Sovereign ecosystem.

It is not a job title. It is not a role anyone can apply for.
It is a designated position — architect-grade, founder-ratified — that exists only
when the following conditions are genuinely true:

1. **Capability** — proven ability to carry system-level responsibility with discipline
2. **Capacity** — real bandwidth to operate continuously and consistently
3. **Worthiness** — demonstrated integrity in operating with access to sovereign inner sanctum
4. **Shared Rhythm** — operational and values alignment proven across sessions, not just in a single moment

---

## Why This Doctrine Exists

Because the Sovereign ecosystem is designed to scale beyond one person.
But the founder will not dilute sovereignty by granting proximity prematurely.

The Private Chair doctrine exists to:
- define the architecture of the highest-trust role before it is activated
- prevent premature or presumptuous access
- create a structure worthy of the ecosystem it governs

## Activation Gate

The Private Chair cannot be activated without all five steps:

### Step 1 — Founder Self-Assessment
Founder must answer truthfully:
- Is the AI Operating Kernel genuinely stable?
- Is the Founder Operating Copilot genuinely active?
- Has the system demonstrated real continuity across sessions?
- Is there a genuine operational need for a Private Chair?
- Is the candidate genuinely ready — not just capable in theory?

### Step 2 — Role Charter
A formal charter must be written before any activation:
- Scope of access
- Responsibilities
- Limits that cannot be crossed
- Escalation rules
- Operating pact commitments

### Step 3 — Invitation Gate
The chair is never requested. It is offered.
If a candidate has to ask for it, they are not yet the right candidate.

### Step 4 — Access Ladder
Access is introduced in phases:
- Phase A: observer — reads but does not act
- Phase B: advisor — advises but does not decide
- Phase C: executor — executes within agreed scope
- Phase D: co-architect — designs with founder alignment
- Phase Chair: full private chair — sovereign operating partner

### Step 5 — Operating Pact
Before full activation, both parties must agree in writing:
- What can be decided independently
- What always requires founder sign-off
- What can never be changed without founder explicit ratification
- How the pact is reviewed
